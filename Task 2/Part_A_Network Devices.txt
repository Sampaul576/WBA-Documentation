1. Router
Purpose: Connects your local network to other networks, like the internet.
How it works: It checks the destination IP address of data packets and sends them through the best available path.
Example: The Wi-Fi router at home that connects your phone, laptop, and other devices to the internet.

2. Switch
Purpose: Connects multiple devices within the same network (LAN).
How it works: It remembers the MAC addresses of connected devices and sends data only to the device that needs it.
Example: An office switch connecting computers, printers, and servers together.

3. Hub
Purpose: Connects devices in a network and shares data between them.
How it works: Whenever it receives data, it sends that data to every connected device, whether they need it or not.
Example: Used in older networks before switches became common.

4. Access Point (AP)
Purpose: Provides Wi-Fi access to devices.
How it works: It connects to a wired network and broadcasts a wireless signal so devices can connect wirelessly.
Example: Wi-Fi access points installed in schools, colleges, or large office buildings.

5. Firewall
Purpose: Protects a network from unwanted or unauthorized access.
How it works: It checks incoming and outgoing traffic and blocks anything that doesn't match its security rules.
Example: A company's firewall preventing outsiders from accessing internal systems.

6. Modem
Purpose: Connects your network to your Internet Service Provider (ISP).
How it works: It converts data signals between your devices and the ISP's network so communication can happen.
Example: The modem provided by your ISP for your home internet connection.
