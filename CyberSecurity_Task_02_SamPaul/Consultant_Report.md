# Cybersecurity Consultant Report — College Security Posture Assessment

**Prepared for:** College Administration
**Prepared by:** Security Analyst (Internship Project)
**Subject:** Improving the College's Cybersecurity Posture

---

## Executive Summary

This report presents a brief assessment of the college's current cybersecurity risk landscape and provides actionable recommendations to strengthen its security posture, based on a structured risk assessment of key institutional assets.

## Top 5 Risks

1. **Data Breach of Student Records** — High risk due to sensitive personal and academic data being a target for attackers.
2. **Examination Data Leakage or Tampering** — Threatens academic integrity if compromised before exams.
3. **Network Infrastructure Compromise** — A single point of failure that, if breached, could disrupt every dependent system.
4. **Phishing Attacks on Staff/Students** — Likely entry point for larger breaches due to human error.
5. **Unpatched Systems and Software** — Leaves known vulnerabilities open to exploitation by malware and ransomware.

## Top 5 Recommendations

1. **Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** across all staff and student accounts.
2. **Establish a regular patch management schedule** for all systems and software.
3. **Conduct periodic security awareness training** for staff and students to reduce phishing risk.
4. **Segment the network**, isolating critical systems (exam servers, records databases) from general access networks.
5. **Set up automated, regularly tested backups** for all critical data.

## Expected Benefits

- **Reduced risk of data breaches** affecting student and faculty records.
- **Improved resilience** against ransomware and malware through backups and patching.
- **Stronger first line of defense** through trained, security-aware staff and students.
- **Faster recovery** from incidents due to backups and a clear incident response process.
- **Increased trust** from students, parents, and faculty in the college's ability to protect their data.
