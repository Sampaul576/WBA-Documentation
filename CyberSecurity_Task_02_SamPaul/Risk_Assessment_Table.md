# Risk Assessment Table — College Security Analyst Activity

As a Security Analyst for the college, the following 5 assets were identified along with their associated threats, impact, and risk level.

| Asset | Threat | Impact | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Student Records | Data Breach | High — exposes personal/academic data, legal liability | **High** |
| Examination Data | Leakage / Tampering before exams | High — compromises exam fairness and institutional credibility | **High** |
| College Website | Defacement / DDoS Attack | Medium — disrupts public access, reputational damage | **Medium** |
| Faculty Information | Unauthorized Access | Medium — exposes personal and payroll data | **Medium** |
| Network Infrastructure | Malware / Unauthorized Access | High — can disrupt all connected systems and services | **High** |

### Risk Level Rationale

- **Student Records (High):** Contains sensitive personal identifiable information (PII); a breach would have legal and reputational consequences.
- **Examination Data (High):** A leak before exams could compromise the integrity of the entire academic process.
- **College Website (Medium):** Public-facing, but typically does not store highly sensitive data — impact is mostly reputational/availability-based.
- **Faculty Information (Medium):** Sensitive, but a smaller dataset compared to the full student body.
- **Network Infrastructure (High):** A single point of failure — if compromised, it affects every other asset that depends on it.
